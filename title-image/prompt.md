Photorealistic cinematic frame, 16:9 anamorphic widescreen, in the visual
style of Roger Deakins (1917, Blade Runner 2049): shallow depth of field,
soft volumetric haze, subtle heat shimmer, fine natural film grain,
deliberate light hierarchy with deep but not crushed shadows.

SETTING — A small Renaissance-era bronze foundry, late at night. The air
is heavy with soot, drifting smoke, and the dim red ambient glow of a
distant forge at the far right edge of frame. A weathered, ash-blackened
oak workbench dominates the lower portion. The stone wall behind is rough,
scorched, lit only indirectly. A few iron tools — tongs, a long skimmer —
hang in soft focus on the wall, suggested rather than detailed.

COMPOSITION (medium shot) —

Upper left: a heavy, soot-blackened bronze crucible hangs from a thick
iron chain entering frame from the top-left corner. A single bare human
hand — large, calloused, knuckles black with carbon dust, dirt under the
fingernails, weathered skin, no glove — grips the chain firmly. Only the
hand and a sliver of forearm are visible at the left edge. The crucible
is tilted, pouring.

Center vertical axis: the pour. A continuous stream of molten matter
falls from crucible to mold. The TOP of the stream consists of dense,
legible C source code written in luminous glowing characters —
recognizable tokens like pthread_create, cache_entry_t *entry,
queue_push(...), pointer asterisks, braces and semicolons, all glowing
white-hot yellow as if the glyphs themselves are the heat source. The
MIDDLE of the stream shows the glyphs softening, smearing, running
together, color shifting from white-yellow toward incandescent orange.
The BOTTOM of the stream is pure, viscous, glowing molten bronze — no
glyphs remain.

Lower right: a rectangular clay casting mold, roughly the size of a
single book page, sits open-topped on the workbench. Its inner flat
surface bears deeply engraved Gherkin text in negative relief — crisp,
hand-cut depressions reading exactly:

  Scenario: 5 clients simultaneously APPEND
            distinct messages and all are stored
    Given an IMAP server with a custom storage backend
      And 5 IMAP connections authenticated
    When all 5 connections APPEND a distinct
         512KB attachment to the "INBOX"
    Then all 5 APPENDs return OK
     And the INBOX contains exactly 5 messages

As bronze settles into these depressions, the keywords Scenario, Given,
When, Then glow most brilliantly — incandescent white-yellow, almost
photonic — while the surrounding clay remains warm red-orange.

LIGHTING HIERARCHY —
- Brightest point (the visual anchor): the inside of the mold, where
  bronze fills the engraved Gherkin keywords. White-yellow incandescence.
- Secondary: the falling stream itself.
- Tertiary: scattered sparks where the stream meets the bronze pool,
  embers floating upward.
- Ambient: the distant forge at frame edge — deep warm red.
- Everything else: sooty darkness, rough textures lit only by reflection.

ATMOSPHERE — heavy drifting smoke in the upper third of frame, fine
sparks rising from the impact point, visible heat shimmer above the mold,
particulate dust suspended in the air, slight motion blur on the falling

PALETTE — deep charcoal blacks, sooty browns, weathered stone gray, warm
orange-red mid-tones, brilliant white-yellow at the anchor point. Almost
monochromatic in the shadows; the only saturated color is the heat
itself.

TEXTURE — visible patina and corrosion on the iron chain, deep grain on
the oak workbench, pitted clay surface on the mold, viscous flow on the
molten bronze, gritty texture on the human hand.

MOOD — reverent, focused, ancient craft meeting precise modern
specification. A moment of transformation. The workshop is genuinely
dirty; the act of casting itself is sacred.

NEGATIVE — no text outside the mold engraving and the falling code
stream, no watermarks, no signatures, no logos, no UI elements, no
decorative borders, no overlay title.
